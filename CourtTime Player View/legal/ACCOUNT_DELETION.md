# Account Deletion

**Effective date:** [EFFECTIVE DATE]

> ⚠️ **DRAFT — Requires legal review before publication.**

This page explains how to delete your CourtTime account, what data is removed, and what is retained.

Both Apple App Store and Google Play Store require apps that allow account creation to also offer in-app account deletion. We honor that requirement. Deleting your account is free, requires no explanation, and can be done at any time.

---

## How to delete your account

You have three ways to delete your account.

### From the mobile app (recommended)

1. Open the CourtTime mobile app and sign in.
2. Tap **Profile** → **Settings** → **Delete Account**.
3. Confirm the deletion when prompted.

### From the web app

1. Sign in at [courttimeapp.com](https://courttimeapp.com).
2. Open your profile menu and choose **Delete Account**.
3. Confirm the deletion.

### By email

Email **reidbissell@courttimeapp.com** from the email address on your account with the subject line "Delete my account." We will verify your identity and process the deletion within 30 days. We may ask you to confirm the request from a second device or via a verification link before we proceed.

---

## What gets deleted

When you delete your account, we permanently remove the following from our active systems:

- Your account credentials (email, password hash, JWT sessions).
- Your profile information (name, phone, mailing address, gender, skill level, USTA rating, bio, profile photo).
- Your messages with other members (both sent and received copies).
- Your bulletin board posts and drill / event sign-ups.
- Your push notification tokens (no further notifications will be delivered).
- Your notification preferences.
- Your facility membership requests and active memberships.
- Strike, lockout, and Terms & Conditions acceptance records you wish to clear, except where retention is required (see below).

---

## What is retained, and why

Some information is retained after account deletion for legitimate operational, legal, or safety reasons:

| Item | Retained for | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Anonymized booking history** | Indefinitely | Facility administrators rely on aggregate booking statistics to operate their courts. Your bookings are stripped of personal identifiers. |
| **Financial records** | 7 years | U.S. tax law requires retention of transaction records (applies only to facility administrators who paid for CourtTime via Stripe). |
| **Records subject to legal hold** | As required by law | If we have an active legal obligation to preserve specific records (e.g. response to a subpoena), we retain them until the obligation ends. |
| **Encrypted backups** | Up to 30 days | Production database backups containing your data are purged on the next full backup rotation cycle. |
| **Server logs** | Up to 90 days | High-level request and error logs containing IP addresses are aged out automatically. |
| **Abuse / safety records** | As needed | If your account was suspended for harassment or other terms violations, a record of the suspension may be retained to enforce facility-level bans. |

We do **not** retain your messages, profile content, or contact information after deletion.

---

## Timeline

- **Immediate** — your account is deactivated, push notifications stop, and you can no longer log in.
- **Within 30 days** — your data is purged from active production systems.
- **Within 60 days** — your data is purged from rotating backups.

---

## Effect on memberships and bookings

- **Active memberships** — deleting your account also cancels your memberships at all facilities you belong to. Your facility administrators will see that the membership has ended.
- **Future bookings** — any future court reservations under your account are cancelled and the slots are released.
- **Past bookings** — anonymized records remain for facility statistics. Personal identifiers are removed.
- **Outstanding fees** — any facility fees, fines, or other obligations owed to your facility are between you and the facility. Deleting your CourtTime account does not discharge those obligations. Resolve any outstanding matters with the facility before deleting.

---

## Re-creating your account

If you change your mind, you can sign up again at any time using the same email address. A new account will not restore your previous bookings, messages, profile, or facility memberships — those are gone permanently.
